Misinformation/Satire AI Fact-Check Helper

The Misinformation/Satire AI will help fact checkers and readers distinguish between two types of content that are commonly confused - nefarious fake news and protected-speech satire. It will automatically classify stories as Humorous/Satirical vs. Questionable/Abusive to improve the efficiency of the fact-checking workflow.
The classification model relies on psycho-linguistic features that aim to capture humorous aspects often associated with satirical content.
The results of the classification will be automatically aggregated into a weekly newsletter which will help to increase the distribution of fact-checks to new and wider audiences.

The Technology - Story Prediction and Insights

Social Media stories are scanned by the model and classified on a scale between Humorous/Satirical and Questionable/Abusive

Questionable/Abusive ContentUnknownHumorous/Satirical Content

Following are insights regarding the psycho-linguistic cues that helped to derive the model prediction

Incongruity

45% likely

Semantic relatedness between the first sentence and the last sentence, as a method of punchline identification

Semantic Validity

19% likely

Contextual imbalances between named entities introduced in the final sentence compared to the rest of the story

Sentiment Amplifiers

11% likely

Based on punctuation such as quotes, exclamation mark, and more

Readability

10% likely

Flesch–Kincaid readability formula based on counts of syllables, words, and sentences.

Modality

7% likely

Grammatical mood refers to the use of auxiliary verbs and adverbs to express uncertainty

Toxicity

4% likely

Demeaning and abusive language in general.

Identifying Nuances in Fake News vs. Satire: Using Semantic and Linguistic Cues - Levi et al, EMNLP 2019
